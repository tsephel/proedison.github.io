Frequently Asked Questions

Basic Q&A we get frequently.

What are the financial benefits of solar energy?

Installing a solar energy system on your property can save you money on your electricity bills and protect you against future rate increases. The amount you save will depend on solar policies and utility rates in your area, but switching to solar is a wise investment regardless of your location.

What are the environmental advantages of solar energy?

Like other renewable energy sources, solar power has many environmental and health benefits. Using solar energy helps to reduce greenhouse gas emissions that contribute to climate change and leads to less air pollution, such as sulfur dioxide and particulate matter, which can cause health problems.

What is net metering and how does it work?

Net metering is a method that utilities use to credit owners of solar energy systems for the electricity generated by their solar panels. With net metering, you only pay for electricity usage beyond what your solar panels produce. Policies for net metering vary from state to state, so it's important to research them beforehand.

How do solar photovoltaic (PV) panels work?

During the day, solar panels absorb the sun's energy and convert it into direct current (DC) electricity. Since most homes and businesses use alternating current (AC) electricity, the DC electricity goes through an inverter to convert it into usable AC electricity. You can then either consume the electricity on your property or send it back to the electric grid.

How much does solar panel maintenance cost?

Solar panel systems consist of durable tempered glass and typically require minimal maintenance for the 25 to 35 years that they generate power. In most cases, frequent cleaning of your solar panels is not necessary. If any issues arise, most equipment manufacturers offer warranties, although the warranty terms differ among companies.

What solar energy incentives and rebates are available?

The availability of solar rebates and incentives varies depending on your location. The most significant incentive is the 30% federal investment tax credit (ITC), which allows you to deduct 30% of the cost of your solar energy system from your taxes. Certain states offer additional tax credits, and some municipalities and utilities provide cash rebates or other incentives.

What are the financing options for solar energy?

You have three options for financing your solar energy system: purchasing it with cash, taking out a solar loan to buy it, or signing a solar lease or power purchase agreement (PPA). EnergySage's Instant Estimate Tool can help you determine the costs and savings over 20 years of each financing option based on real quote data from your area.

Should I buy or lease my solar panel system?

The decision to buy or lease your solar energy system depends on your reasons for switching to solar. If you want to maximize the financial returns of your solar energy system, buying the system is likely the better option. However, if you prioritize an easy, maintenance-free way to reduce your energy bills and help the environment, you should consider a solar lease.
